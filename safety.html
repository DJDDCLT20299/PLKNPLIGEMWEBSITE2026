Safety

Responsible research practices for protein engineering and e-waste handling

Laboratory Safety

All experiments were conducted in BSL-1 certified laboratories. Our safety protocols included:

  • All team members completed mandatory biosafety and chemical safety training
  • Standard PPE (lab coats, nitrile gloves, safety glasses) worn at all times
  • Chemical handling: terbium chloride (TbCl₃) and other REE salts handled in fume hood with proper waste collection
  • Acid digestion of lamp phosphors (2 M HCl, 80°C) performed in fume hood with acid-resistant PPE
  • Recombinant protein work conducted in dedicated BSL-1 workspace with bleach decontamination
  • All liquid and solid biohazard waste autoclaved before disposal
  • Chemical waste (REE solutions, acids) collected separately for licensed disposal

E-Waste and Environmental Safety

Fluorescent lamp phosphors contain trace mercury, requiring careful handling. All e-waste processing followed institutional hazardous waste protocols:

  • Lamp phosphors sourced from certified e-waste recycler (already de-mercurated)
  • Additional mercury testing (ICP-MS) confirmed <0.1 ppm Hg in processed leachate
  • Process designed to minimize chemical waste: protein-based, water-phase system avoids organic solvents entirely
  • End-of-life LanM protein is biodegradable; no persistent environmental accumulation

Biosecurity and Dual-Use

Our project uses only non-pathogenic chassis organisms (Risk Group 1) and synthesizes proteins that bind rare earth elements — no toxic proteins, virulence factors, or biologically active molecules are expressed. We have carefully considered dual-use implications:

  • The LanM protein binds REEs with high affinity but does not alter host physiology
  • No antibiotic resistance markers that could transfer to human pathogens (all markers are for E. coli-specific plasmid maintenance)
  • The tryptophan sensitization mechanism is purely optical — no genetic or chemical modification to living organisms is required for the detection system
  • Our technology promotes environmental sustainability by enabling e-waste recycling, reducing the need for REE mining

Safety Statement

Our project poses minimal biosafety or biosecurity risk. All work was conducted under institutional biosafety committee oversight.
