Engineering

Our iterative Design-Build-Test-Learn cycle for LanM variant development

Engineering Cycle Overview

We followed the iGEM Design-Build-Test-Learn (DBTL) cycle across three major iterations to engineer, characterize, and validate our tryptophan-sensitized lanmodulin variants for selective terbium recovery.

📐 Design

Structural analysis of LanM (PDB: 6MHT), identification of Trp substitution sites within 8 Å of Tb³⁺ binding pocket, and in silico mutagenesis screening.

🧪 Build

Gene synthesis (Twist Bioscience), Gibson assembly into pET-28a(+), transformation into E. coli BL21(DE3), and protein expression/purification.

📊 Test

Tb³⁺ binding affinity (ITC), luminescence spectroscopy (λex=280 nm, λem=545 nm), selectivity assays against competing metals, and e-waste leachate tests.

📝 Learn

Each iteration informed the next: from single variants to combinatorial optimization, from buffer conditions to real-world leachate testing.

Engineering Iterations

Iteration 1: Single Variant Screening

Design: Three single Trp variants (D26W, N58W, D102W) designed based on LanM crystal structure. Each targets a different EF-hand domain.

Build: Genes synthesized (Twist Bioscience), cloned into pET-28a(+), expressed in E. coli BL21(DE3) with IPTG induction (0.5 mM, 18°C, 16 h). Proteins purified by Ni-NTA affinity chromatography with >95% purity by SDS-PAGE.

Test: Tb³⁺ binding measured by tryptophan fluorescence quenching (λex 280 nm, λem 340 nm). Sensitized Tb luminescence measured at 545 nm. All three variants showed Tb-dependent luminescence, with N58W showing the highest signal-to-noise ratio.

Learn: Position N58 in EF-hand 2 provides optimal Trp-Tb distance (~6 Å) and orientation for energy transfer. Single variants give moderate luminescence — combinatorial variants may enhance signal through additive energy transfer.

Iteration 2: Combinatorial Variants & Optimization

Design: Double (D26W/N58W, N58W/D102W) and triple (D26W/N58W/D102W) variants designed. Additional optimization: pH range (1.5–7.0), buffer composition, and Tb³⁺ concentration range (1 nM – 100 µM).

Build: Same expression and purification protocol as Iteration 1. Protein yields: 8–15 mg/L culture.

Test: Double variant D26W/N58W showed 2.3-fold enhanced luminescence vs. N58W alone. Triple variant showed only 1.5-fold enhancement, suggesting positional interference. Optimal pH: 2.5–4.0. Limit of detection (LOD): 3 ppb Tb³⁺.

Learn: Double Trp substitution provides synergistic energy transfer. pH 2.5 is ideal for e-waste leachate compatibility. Luminescence signal is linear from 10 ppb to 10 ppm Tb³⁺.

Iteration 3: Real E-Waste Validation

Design: Validation with real fluorescent lamp phosphor leachate. Additional modifications: SpyTag fusion for magnetic bead immobilization (capture system).

Build: D26W/N58W variant expressed with C-terminal SpyTag. Immobilized on SpyCatcher-magnetic agarose beads (50 µL beads per 1 mL lysate).

Test: Leachate from crushed fluorescent lamp phosphors (HCl, pH 2.5) containing Tb, Eu, Y, La, Ce, and transition metals (Fe, Cu, Zn). LanM-SpyTag beads captured 99% Tb³⁺ with minimal co-binding of other REEs or transition metals. Elution with 50 mM citrate, pH 1.5 recovered 85% bound Tb. Beads reused over 10 cycles with <10% activity loss.

Learn: Protein-based Tb recovery from real e-waste is feasible and selective. Multiple variants are contributed as iGEM BioBricks for future REE recovery applications.

Computational Modeling

To guide our design, we performed molecular dynamics simulations of WT and variant LanM structures. Key findings:

  • Trp rotamer analysis: The N58W substitution adopts a stable rotamer with the indole ring oriented toward the Tb³⁺ binding pocket (Trp-Nε–Tb distance: 5.8 ± 0.4 Å).
  • Binding energy calculations: MM-GBSA calculations predicted ΔΔG of −1.2 kcal/mol for N58W relative to WT, consistent with preserved or slightly enhanced Tb affinity.
  • pH stability: Constant-pH MD simulations at pH 2.5 showed that the EF-hand scaffold remains folded, with key coordinating residues (D, E, N) maintaining their side-chain conformations.
