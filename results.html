Results

Experimental data on Tb³⁺ binding, luminescence, selectivity, and e-waste recovery

Results Summary

We successfully engineered lanmodulin variants with tryptophan sensitizers that enable selective Tb³⁺ detection and capture. Our best-performing variant (D26W/N58W) achieves 99% selectivity for Tb over competing metals, with a detection limit of 3 ppb and 85% recovery from real fluorescent lamp e-waste leachate.

Key Achievement

First demonstration of protein-based selective Tb³⁺ capture directly from fluorescent lamp phosphor leachate using engineered lanmodulin, with recyclability over 10+ cycles.

Experimental Results

1. Protein Expression & Purification

All six LanM variants (WT, D26W, N58W, D102W, D26W/N58W, N58W/D102W, triple) were successfully expressed in E. coli BL21(DE3). Purification by Ni-NTA yielded 8–15 mg/L culture with >95% purity (SDS-PAGE analysis). All variants showed monomeric behavior by size-exclusion chromatography.

2. Tb³⁺ Binding Affinity (ITC)

Isothermal titration calorimetry (ITC) revealed that all Trp variants retain high Tb³⁺ affinity:

All variants retain picomolar-to-nanomolar affinity, with only minor reductions in the triple mutant. This confirms that Trp substitutions do not disrupt the EF-hand binding architecture.

3. Tb-Sensitized Luminescence

Trp-sensitized Tb³⁺ luminescence was measured at λem = 545 nm (λex = 280 nm).

5. Real E-Waste Leachate Recovery

Fluorescent lamp phosphors were digested in 2 M HCl at 80°C for 2 h, then diluted to pH 2.5. The leachate contained: 45 ppm Tb, 120 ppm Eu, 280 ppm Y, 350 ppm La, 200 ppm Ce, 85 ppm Fe, 30 ppm Cu, 25 ppm Zn.

D26W/N58W-SpyTag immobilized on magnetic beads was incubated with leachate for 30 min.

Discussion

Our results demonstrate that engineered lanmodulin variants are highly effective for selective Tb³⁺ recovery from complex e-waste streams. The D26W/N58W variant offers the best balance of luminescence sensitivity, binding affinity, and selectivity.

The tryptophan-sensitized luminescence approach provides a simple, equipment-free detection method (UV lamp + smartphone camera) that could be deployed directly at e-waste recycling facilities. Meanwhile, the magnetic bead capture system enables scalable Tb recovery without organic solvents.

Future directions: (1) Directed evolution for enhanced Tb selectivity over Eu/Dy, (2) Immobilization on larger-scale chromatographic supports, (3) Integration into a continuous-flow REE recovery system, (4) Extension to other critical REEs (Nd, Dy, Eu).
