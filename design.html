Design

Rational engineering of tryptophan-sensitized lanmodulin variants for Tb³⁺ detection and capture

Design Overview

Our design strategy focuses on introducing tryptophan (Trp) residues at specific positions within lanmodulin's EF-hand binding loops to enable Tb³⁺ detection via the "antenna effect" — where Trp absorbs UV light and transfers the energy to bound Tb³⁺, producing characteristic green luminescence at 545 nm.

Design Principles

1. Rationally Select Trp Positions

Using the crystal structure of LanM (PDB: 6MHT), we identified residues within 8 Å of the Tb³⁺ binding site in EF-hands 1–3. Positions D26, N58, and D102 were chosen for Trp substitution — close enough for efficient energy transfer but not directly coordinating the metal ion.

2. Variant Library Design

We created three single variants (D26W, N58W, D102W), two double variants (D26W/N58W, N58W/D102W), and one triple variant (D26W/N58W/D102W). This library allows us to probe the contribution of each position to Tb binding and sensitization.

3. Fusion Tag Construction

Each variant was fused with an N-terminal His₆-tag for Ni-NTA purification and a C-terminal SpyTag for potential surface immobilization. A TEV protease cleavage site between His₆ and LanM enables tag removal for downstream applications.

Genetic Construct Design

All variant genes were codon-optimized for E. coli expression and cloned into pET-28a(+) vector under the T7/lac promoter system.

Biosensor Design: Tryptophan-Sensitized Luminescence

The core innovation of our design is the Trp-sensitized Tb³⁺ luminescence mechanism. When tryptophan (λex = 280 nm) is excited by UV light, it undergoes a singlet-to-triplet intersystem crossing. The triplet state of Trp then transfers energy to the excited state of Tb³⁺ (⁷F₆ → ⁵D₄ transition), which relaxes by emitting green light at 545 nm.

Key Design Principle

The energy transfer efficiency depends critically on the Trp-Tb³⁺ distance (optimal < 8 Å) and the orientation of the Trp indole ring relative to the metal center. Our rational design strategy ensures both criteria are met through strategic placement in the EF-hand loops.

For the capture system (non-luminescent), we use the same LanM variants immobilized on magnetic agarose beads via the SpyTag/SpyCatcher system. This allows magnetic separation of Tb-bound protein from solution, followed by low-pH elution to recover pure Tb³⁺.
