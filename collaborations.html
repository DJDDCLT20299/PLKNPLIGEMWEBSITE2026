Collaborations

Working together with other iGEM teams and research partners

iGEM Team Collaborations

🧪 Team UniLausanne — REE Part Collection

What we did: Shared LanM variant sequences and characterization data with the UniLausanne iGEM team, who were developing a curli fiber display system for REE recovery. We contributed our D26W/N58W variant to their part collection.

Outcome: Joint publication of 4 LanM-based REE binding parts in the iGEM Registry. Cross-validation of Tb binding affinity between our ITC data and their whole-cell binding assays.

🧪 Team TU-Delft — Biosensor Workshop

What we did: Co-hosted a virtual workshop on "Protein-Based Biosensors for Environmental Monitoring" with TU-Delft. Shared our Trp-sensitization approach; they shared their FRET-based heavy metal sensor designs.

Outcome: Comparison of Trp-sensitized vs. FRET-based lanthanide detection. Joint poster at iGEM Meetup.

🧪 Team NPL-HongKong — E-Waste Collection

What we did: Partnered with a local Hong Kong iGEM team to co-organize a city-wide fluorescent lamp e-waste collection drive. We provided educational materials on REE recycling; they provided collection logistics.

Outcome: 200 kg of fluorescent lamps collected and properly recycled. Joint social media campaign reached 10K+ people.

External Partnerships

🏭 E-Waste Recyclers Association (HK)

Provided access to de-mercurated fluorescent lamp phosphors, shared industry requirements for pH/acidity tolerance, and offered feedback on scalability of the protein-based approach.

🔬 Twist Bioscience

Sponsored gene synthesis for all 7 LanM variants. Provided express synthesis service with codon optimization for E. coli expression.

🧪 Integrated DNA Technologies (IDT)

Supplied gBlocks for Gibson assembly and primers for colony PCR screening.

🧬 LanM-Tb

Engineered lanmodulin variants for selective terbium recovery from fluorescent lamp e-waste leachate.
